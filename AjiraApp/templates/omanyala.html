Ferdinand Omanyala Omurwa is a Kenyan sprinter and the African 100m record holder (9.77s). He made a remarkable transition from rugby to become one of the world's top sprinters through discipline, dedication, and rapid development.

About Ferdinand

From rugby player to Africa's fastest man - the remarkable journey of a trailblazing athlete

Early Career

Omanyala initially played rugby at the University of Nairobi before transitioning to sprinting. His natural speed was quickly recognized, and he began focusing on track events in 2016.

Despite limited resources and training facilities compared to international competitors, Omanyala's dedication and raw talent propelled him to rapid success on the continental and global stage.

Impact & Legacy

Omanyala has revolutionized sprinting in Kenya and across Africa, proving that the continent can produce world-class sprinters to compete with traditional powerhouses like Jamaica and the United States.

His success has inspired a new generation of African sprinters and brought unprecedented attention to track and field in the region.

Career Highlights

Key moments in Omanyala's remarkable sprinting career

  • 2021
    9.77s — African Record

    Set at the Absa Kip Keino Classic in Nairobi, breaking the previous African record and announcing his arrival on the world stage.

  • 2022
    Commonwealth Games Gold

    Won the 100m gold medal at the Commonwealth Games in Birmingham, defeating a strong field of international sprinters.

  • 2022
    African Championships

    Crowned 100m champion at the African Athletics Championships in Mauritius, solidifying his status as Africa's premier sprinter.

  • 2023
    World Championships

    Reached the semifinals at the World Athletics Championships in Budapest, competing against the world's best sprinters.
